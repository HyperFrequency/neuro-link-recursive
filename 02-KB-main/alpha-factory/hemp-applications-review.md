# Applications of Hemp in Textiles, Paper, Insulation, and Beyond

## Overview
Crini, Lichtfouse, Chaabane, and Morin-Crini (2020) review industrial hemp (Cannabis sativa L.) applications across textiles, paper, insulation, building materials, horticulture, animal nutrition, food, nutraceuticals, cosmetics, medicine, agrochemistry, energy production, and environmental remediation. The review documents >250 application categories and argues hemp is a near-unique multi-functional crop supporting bio-economy transitions.

## Conceptual Model
Hemp's economic versatility stems from three raw-material streams: bast fibers (outer stem, long cellulose fibers), hurd/shives (inner stem, woody core), and seeds (oil, protein, fiber). Each stream supports distinct end-use industries, with agronomic co-production enabling comprehensive crop utilization. Low input requirements (minimal pesticides/herbicides, drought tolerant, short rotation) and high biomass yields give strong environmental and economic fundamentals for bio-based materials.

## Details
**Fiber applications**:
- Textiles: apparel, ropes, canvas, geotextiles; blends with cotton, linen, wool
- Paper: hemp pulp less requiring of bleaching than wood pulp; historically used for currency, books
- Nonwovens: automotive interiors (BMW, Mercedes use hemp composites), insulation mats

**Hurd/shive applications**:
- Construction: hempcrete (hurd + lime binder) — carbon-negative insulation + load-bearing aggregate with vapor permeability and fire resistance
- Animal bedding: absorbent, low dust, antimicrobial
- Particle board, MDF

**Seed-based**:
- Food: hemp seeds (shelled, hearts), protein powders (30–35% protein), oil (high in $\omega$-3 ALA and $\gamma$-linolenic acid)
- Cosmetics: oil in soaps, creams, hair products
- Nutraceuticals: seed-derived bioactives

**Cannabinoid pharmaceuticals** (outside food): CBD isolates and full-spectrum products for pharmaceutical and wellness applications — note this review focuses on industrial (low-THC) hemp with regulatory-defined THC cap (<0.3% US, <0.2% EU).

**Environmental applications**:
- Phytoremediation: hemp extracts heavy metals (Cd, Pb, Ni, Cu) from contaminated soils
- Carbon sequestration: rapid biomass accumulation (~15 t/ha in 4 months)
- Soil health: deep tap root breaks compaction; rotation crop benefits

**Agrochemistry**: compost, biofuels (bioethanol from fermentation of hurd, biodiesel from seed oil), biogas.

**Regulatory context** (2020 snapshot): EU Common Agricultural Policy distinguishes industrial hemp from cannabis; US 2018 Farm Bill removed industrial hemp from Controlled Substances Act; rapid market growth projected, but supply-chain constraints (processing capacity, certified seed availability) limited scaling.

**Comparison with other fiber crops**: per-hectare yield of hemp fiber (~1–2 t/ha) compares favorably to flax (~1 t/ha) and cotton (~0.5 t/ha) without pesticides. Water use is 50% of cotton.

**Economic viability** (2020 data): market prices volatile; CBD boom distorted supply toward cannabinoid extraction at expense of fiber/seed. Subsequent market corrections restored fiber/seed focus.

**Investment angle for alpha-factory**: agricultural commodity, ESG/sustainability themes, regional policy plays (legalization waves), bio-materials supply-chain verticals.

## Applications
Sustainable building materials (hempcrete in net-zero construction), bio-composite manufacturing, sustainable textile supply, environmental remediation services, specialty food and nutraceutical ingredients.

## Open Questions
- Regional supply-chain bottlenecks (decortication facilities)
- Policy evolution (THC limits, FDA ruling on CBD food/supplements)
- Carbon-credit monetization for hemp cultivation
